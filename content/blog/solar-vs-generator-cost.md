# Solar Panels vs Generator: Which Is Cheaper in the Long Run?

In Nigeria, where "light" (grid power) is often unpredictable, the battle for reliable energy usually comes down to two choices: the traditional petrol/diesel generator or a modern solar power system.

While generators have been the standard for decades, skyrocketing fuel prices have many homeowners in Lagos and beyond asking: **Is solar actually cheaper?**

Let's look at the numbers.

## Comparison at a Glance

| Feature | Petrol/Diesel Generator | Solar Power System |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Initial Cost** | Low (₦150k – ₦600k) | High (₦800k – ₦3M+) |
| **Fuel Cost** | Constant & Rising | **Zero (₦0)** |
| **Maintenance** | Frequent (Oil, Filters, Repairs) | Minimal (Cleaning Panels) |
| **Noise & Pollution** | High | Silent & Clean |
| **Lifespan** | 3 – 7 Years | 20 – 25 Years (Panels) |

## The "Hidden" Costs of Generators

The generator’s biggest trick is its low entry price. However, once you start using it, the expenses pile up:

1.  **Fuel:** At current petrol prices, running a generator for 8 hours daily can cost between ₦60,000 to ₦150,000 monthly.
2.  **Servicing:** Every 100-200 hours of run-time requires an oil change and filter replacement.
3.  **Engine Wear:** Constant running leads to mechanical failures, eventually requiring a total engine overhaul or replacement every few years.

## The Solar Advantage: Pay Once, Use for Decades

Solar is often described as "pre-paying for your electricity for 25 years."

- **Return on Investment (ROI):** For most Nigerian homes, a solar system pays for itself within **2 to 3 years** simply by eliminating fuel costs.
- **Silent Power:** No noise pollution means better sleep and better relations with your neighbors.
- **24/7 Energy:** Unlike a generator that you only turn on when necessary, a solar system is always working, keeping your fridge running and your security lights on all night.

## The Verdict

If you only need power for 1 hour a month, a generator is cheaper. But if you need reliable power every day—especially for a home office, a small business, or a family home—**Solar is significantly cheaper in the long run.**

While the initial cost is higher, the "fuel" (sunlight) is free, and the maintenance is nearly zero.
